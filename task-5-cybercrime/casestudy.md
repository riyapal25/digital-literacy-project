# Case Study — Identity Theft

## Title
The Aadhaar Data Breach — When Personal Identity Gets Stolen

---

## Introduction
Identity theft is one of the most serious forms of cybercrime
in which a fraudster illegally obtains and uses another
person's personal information — such as their name, Aadhaar
number, bank details, or PAN card — without their knowledge
or consent, typically for financial gain. In India, one of
the most significant and widely reported identity theft
incidents involved the alleged breach of the Aadhaar
biometric database managed by the Unique Identification
Authority of India (UIDAI).

---

## The Incident
In January 2018, the Tribune newspaper reported that a
group of anonymous sellers on WhatsApp were offering
unauthorised access to the Aadhaar database for as little
as ₹500. For an additional ₹300, they would also provide
software that could print fake Aadhaar cards using any
individual's stolen data. The breach potentially exposed
the personal information of over 1.1 billion Indian
citizens registered under the Aadhaar system, including
their names, addresses, phone numbers, and Aadhaar numbers.

---

## How It Happened
The breach was not caused by a direct hack of UIDAI's
central servers. Instead, it exploited a vulnerability
in the access portals used by government-appointed
Village Level Entrepreneurs (VLEs) — local agents
authorised to access Aadhaar data for legitimate purposes.
These login credentials were leaked or sold, allowing
unauthorised individuals to access the database freely.
The fraudsters then monetised this access by selling it
to anyone willing to pay a small fee, making it extremely
easy for criminals to obtain any citizen's personal
identity data within minutes.

---

## Who Was Targeted
The breach affected virtually every Indian citizen enrolled
in the Aadhaar system — approximately 1.1 billion people.
However, the most vulnerable targets were individuals who
were unaware of how their Aadhaar data could be misused,
including students, rural citizens, senior citizens, and
people who had linked their Aadhaar to their bank accounts
and mobile numbers.

---

## Consequences
The consequences of this breach were far reaching. Stolen
Aadhaar data could be used to open fraudulent bank accounts,
apply for loans in someone else's name, obtain fake SIM
cards, and commit financial fraud — all without the victim's
knowledge. Beyond financial loss, victims faced serious
threats to their personal privacy and digital identity.
The incident raised major concerns about data security
in government systems and led to widespread public debate
about the safety of centralised biometric databases.
